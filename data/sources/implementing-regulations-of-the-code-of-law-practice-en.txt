Implementing Regulations of the Code of Law Practice
PART 1: DEFINITIONS
Article 1:
The following words and phrases, wherever mentioned in these Regulations, shall have the meanings assigned thereto, unless the context requires otherwise:
Law: Code of Law Practice.
Regulations: Implementing Regulations of the Code of Law Practice.
Profession: Profession of law practice, as defined in Article 1 of the Law.
Ministry: Ministry of Justice.
Minister: Minister of Justice.
Association: Saudi Bar Association.
Competent Department: The department in the Ministry or Association with jurisdiction over the procedures provided for in these Regulations, in accordance with its regulatory powers.
Competent Employee: An employee of the competent department assigned to perform any of the tasks of such department. 
Lawyer: A person licensed to practice law after his registration in the roll. 
Licensee: A lawyer or a foreign law firm licensed to practice law in accordance with the provisions of the Law. 
Non-Saudi Consultant: A consultant who is registered in the register of non-Saudi consultants. 

PART 2: LAW PRACTICE LICENSING
CHAPTER 1: LICENSE REQUIREMENTS
Article 2:
1. A person with acceptable relevant legal experience as referred to in Article 3(c) of the Law shall include any person who: 
a) receives accredited training at law firms, as stipulated in Article 28 of these Regulations;
b) works as a judge;
c) works in investigation or public prosecution;
d) performs legal services in the public or private sectors, in accordance with the controls set by the Ministry; or
e) teaches Islamic jurisprudence, principles of Islamic jurisprudence, or law at the undergraduate or graduate levels in any accredited college or institution. 
2. Completion of the lawyers' qualification program offered or accredited by the Judicial Training Center shall be considered relevant legal experience for a period equal to the duration of the program.

CHAPTER 2: LICENSING PROCEDURES
Article 3:
1. The Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee may, in performance of its duties, request any documents necessary to verify compliance with the license requirements, and the applicant must submit such documents within a period not exceeding 30 days from the date of notification; otherwise, the application shall be deemed void. 
2. The lawyer shall be registered in the roll of practicing lawyers, and a license to practice the profession shall be issued upon payment of the license fees. 
3. If the application for registration in the roll of practicing lawyers is rejected, the applicant shall be notified accordingly, and he may submit a new application after the grounds for rejection no longer exist.

Article 4:
The roll of practicing lawyers shall include the following: 
1. The lawyer's name and contact information. 
2. The license number and its issuance and expiration dates.
3. Address of place of business.
4. Any other information specified by the competent department. 
The lawyer shall notify the competent department of any change to his information within a period not exceeding 15 days from the date of such change. 

Article 5:
The license renewal application shall be submitted within a period of not less than 180 days and not more than one year from the license expiration date. The application shall include the documents specified by the Committee, and the applicant must sign a form acknowledging the fulfillment of the requirements stipulated in Article 3 of the Law.

CHAPTER 3: LAWYERS REGISTRATION AND ADMISSION COMMITTEE
Article 6:
The Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee shall have a secretary designated pursuant to a decision by its chairman, to be in charge of supervising the Committee’s secretariat, preparing and maintaining its minutes and decisions, making the technical and administrative preparations for the Committee’s work and meetings, and performing any tasks assigned by the Committee’s chairman within the limits of his powers. 

Article 7:
1. The meetings of the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee shall not be deemed valid unless attended by all its members, and they shall be held periodically according to a schedule approved thereby and may be held upon a call by its chairman when the need arises; the Committee’s meetings may be conducted remotely via approved electronic means. 
2. The decisions of the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee shall be passed by a majority vote; a member may state his reservation on the decision along with the grounds therefor in the meeting minutes.

CHAPTER 4: TRANSFER TO THE ROLL OF NON-PRACTICING LAWYERS AND LICENSE EXPIRATION
Article 8:
 A lawyer shall be transferred from the roll of practicing lawyers to the roll of non-practicing lawyers pursuant to a decision by the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee, in the following cases: 
1. If he ceases to practice law for a period exceeding one year, as provided for in Article 9 of these Regulations. 
2. If a final decision suspending him from practicing law is issued. 
3. If he fails to obtain the primary membership of the Saudi Bar Association as provided for in Article 21 bis of the Law, or fails to renew his membership.
4. If he engages in any of the activities referred to in Article 17(1) of the Regulations while practicing law.

Article 9:
1. A lawyer who chooses to suspend his practice for a period exceeding one year must submit a request to cancel his license or to transfer his name to the roll of non-practicing lawyers 30 days prior to the date of suspension. 
2. The lawyer requesting suspension shall be transferred to the roll of non-practicing lawyers if:
a) the remaining term of the license is more than one year from the date of the suspension request;
b) he is not under investigation nor is he a subject of a disciplinary action; or
c) he submits proof of conclusion of services or cases or submits proof of reaching an arrangement with clients regarding pending services or cases. If he is a partner or shareholder in a professional company, the lawyer must submit proof of the measures taken by such company regarding the suspension of his practice, and proof of assigning the services to other licensed partners or shareholders, in accordance with relevant laws.

Article 10:
A lawyer whose name has been transferred to the roll of non-practicing lawyers may request from the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee to reinstate his name in the roll of practicing lawyers if the grounds therefor no longer exist.

Article 11:
The license of the lawyer shall terminate and his name shall be stricken off the roll pursuant to a decision by the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee in the following cases: 
1. A request is made by the lawyer to cancel the license.
2. Non-renewal of the license upon expiration of its term. 
3. Issuance of a final decision to strike off the name of the lawyer from the roll and cancel his license. 
4. Non-fulfillment of any of the licensing requirements provided for in the Law, provided that the non-fulfillment is not the result of a disciplinary violation.
5. The lawyer’s death.

Article 12:
The competent department shall notify the lawyer if his name is stricken off the roll and his license is cancelled, or if his name is transferred to the roll of non-practicing lawyers.

PART 3: LAWYERS’ PROFESSIONAL DUTIES AND OBLIGATIONS
CHAPTER 1: GENERAL DUTIES
Article 13:
A lawyer shall, within 90 days from the date of issuance of his license, establish an office suitable for practicing law, and he shall notify the competent department of the national address of the office and any change to such address within 15 days from the date of establishing the office or from the occurrence of such change; a lawyer may establish one or more branch offices. 

Article 14:
A lawyer shall state his name and the number and date of his license in his documents and correspondence as well as in any documents issued in relation to professional services rendered thereby, and he shall display a copy of the license conspicuously in his office.

Article 15:
A lawyer shall provide the competent department with any information or reports requested thereby for the purpose of verifying the lawyer’s compliance with the provisions of the Law, these Regulations, and the Rules of Professional Conduct for Lawyers. 

Article 16:
A lawyer may not be a partner in more than one professional law company, nor may he be employed by more than one law office or professional law company.

Article 17:
1. A lawyer may not combine law practice with any of the following: 
a) Employment in the public sector. 
b) Employment in the private sector, excluding law offices or professional law companies.
c) Membership in any of the committees referred to in Article 1 of the Law. 
2. Law practice may be combined with the practice of other professions not conflicting with the nature thereof, as determined by the Ministry. 

Article 18:
1. Without prejudice to relevant laws and instructions and the provisions relating to professional law companies, a lawyer may be a founder, partner, or shareholder in any company in which his liability is limited to his contribution to the company’s capital; he may also serve as a chairman or a non-executive member in such company’s board of directors or board of managers, and in the committees of such boards. 
2. Subject to the provisions of Article 17 and paragraph (1) of this Article, a lawyer may not manage a company or assume any executive role for its benefit, including the position of a managing director or chief executive officer or any work that would require an employment contract. 

Article 19:
1. When providing any professional service via an intermediary electronic platform, a lawyer shall comply with the following: 
a) The provisions of the Law, these Regulations, and the Rules of Professional Conduct for Lawyers, as well as relevant laws. 
b) Maintaining the privacy of his clients and the confidentiality of information exchanged through the platform, avoiding actual and potential conflicts of interest, and refraining from conduct unbecoming of the profession.
c) Verifying that the platform through which he provides his services complies with relevant laws, maintains the confidentiality of client information, and does not misuse such information, in accordance with relevant laws and instructions. 
2. A lawyer shall be exempted from the obligation stipulated in paragraph 1(c) of this Article if the intermediary electronic platform through which he provides his services is approved by the Association. 

Article 20:
The provisions of Article 16 of the Law shall apply to a lawyer who accepts representation or provides consultations directly or through another person in a matter that he has previously reviewed, participated in the examination thereof, or provided an opinion thereon in the capacity of a judge, junior judge, or member of one of the committees referred to in Article 1 of the Law. 

CHAPTER 2: DUTIES OF A LAWYER WHO IS A PARTNER OR SHAREHOLDER IN A PROFESSIONAL COMPANY
Article 21:
Lawyers who are partners or shareholders shall, upon the incorporation of a professional company, notify the competent department and provide a copy of the articles of incorporation or articles of association, as the case may be, within a period not exceeding 30 days from the date of incorporation.

Article 22:
Lawyers who are partners or shareholders in the professional company shall, if the name of any of them is stricken off the roll or transferred to the roll of non-practicing lawyers, amend the company's articles of incorporation or articles of association in accordance with the provisions of the Companies Law; re-assign the cases and consultations of the disbarred lawyer to other lawyers who are partners or shareholders; and notify the competent department of the measures taken within a period not exceeding 30 days from the date of removal or transfer of his name.

Article 23:
A lawyer who is a partner or shareholder in a professional company shall promptly notify the competent department if any of the company’s partners or shareholders engages in law practice without a license.

CHAPTER 3: LAWYERS' CONTRACT AND FEES
Article 24:
A lawyer shall, prior to commencing the agreed-upon service, draw up a written contract with his client, and the lawyer’s fees shall be payable as specified in the contract. 

Article 25:
Jurisdiction over disputes concerning fees of lawyers shall be as follows: 
1. If the lawyer’s work with a client pertains to a case considered before a court, jurisdiction shall lie with the court that considered the case. 
2. If the lawyer’s work with a client pertains to a case considered before an entity other than a court, or if such work does not involve a judicial dispute, jurisdiction shall lie with the court having subject-matter jurisdiction in accordance with the relevant statutory provisions.

Article 26:
The client must notify the lawyer in writing upon ending the lawyer’s services.

Article 27:
1. The lawyer’s fees shall be determined by an expert appointed pursuant to a court decision in the cases stipulated in Articles 26, 27, and 28 of the Law, as well as in the event of the client’s death before the lawyer completes the assigned service and no agreement between the lawyer and the heirs is reached regarding the fees.
2. The following shall be taken into account in determining the fees: 
a) Type and nature of the assigned service, the effort exerted, and the benefit received by the client. 
b) Prevailing fees for similar services provided in the local market by comparable lawyers.
c) Effect of the contract on the lawyer’s relationship with other clients. 
d) Nature and duration of the professional relationship. 
e) The lawyer's professional experience and reputation. 

PART 4: TRAINEES
CHAPTER 1: REGISTRATION OF TRAINEES
Article 28:
In order for training in law offices to be considered a relevant legal experience in accordance with Article 2(1) of these Regulations, a trainee must be registered with the competent department by the lawyer for whom he works, and must complete the minimum non-practical training specified by the Ministry in coordination with the Association.

Article 29:
Subject to the provisions of the Law and these Regulations, the relationship between the lawyer and the trainee shall be governed by an employment contract, in accordance with the provisions of the Labor Law. 

Article 30:
For a trainee to be registered, the following requirements must be satisfied: 
1. The trainee meets the requirements for registration in the roll of practicing lawyers, except for the experience requirement. 
2. The lawyer in the law office or a partner in the law firm where the trainee receives training must have no less than three years of relevant legal experience.
3. The trainee's registration period must not exceed four years.
4. The number of trainees in the law office or in the law firm must not exceed the limit set by the competent department.
5. The trainee must not be registered with or employed by another lawyer.

Article 31:
1. The lawyer shall submit the trainee’s application for registration to the competent department within a period not exceeding five days from the date of contracting therewith, accompanied with the documents specified by the competent department. 
2. The applicant shall provide the documents requested by the competent department within a period not exceeding 30 days from the date of notification of the request; otherwise, the application shall be deemed void. 

CHAPTER 2: TRAINEE’S RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS
Article 32:
In order to consider the training as relevant legal experience, the registered trainee shall comply with the following: 
1. The profession’s code of ethics as stipulated in the Law, these Regulations, the Rules of Professional Conduct for Lawyers, and any relevant laws. 
2. Completing the approved training plan and the minimum non-practical training referred to in Article 28 of these Regulations. 
3.Working for the lawyer for not less than the number of hours specified by the Ministry. 
4. Refrain from presenting himself as a lawyer or implying that he is a licensed lawyer. 

Article 33:
The registered trainee may perform all legal services, including litigation and attending interrogation sessions, pursuant to a power of attorney issued by the lawyer, under his supervision and responsibility, and with his signature on all memoranda submitted to the court, unless the client requires the lawyer to personally perform such duties.

CHAPTER 3: TRAINING LAWYER OBLIGATIONS
Article 34:
A lawyer shall supervise and train the trainee; to this end, he shall undertake the following: 
1. Pass knowledge to the trainee and develop a training plan that includes practical and non-practical aspects in accordance with the training plan models prepared by the Ministry in coordination with the Association.
2. Take the necessary measures to ensure that the trainee complies with the Rules of Professional Conduct for Lawyers and with the profession’s code of ethics as stipulated in the Law, these Regulations, and relevant laws. 
3. Supervise the work carried out by the trainee and be responsible for all professional work performed thereby on the lawyer’s behalf. 

PART 5: NON-SAUDI CONSULTANTS
Article 35:
Without prejudice to the provisions of Article 3 of the Law, a licensee may not employ a non-Saudi consultant except after being registered in the register of non-Saudi consultants. 

Article 36:
To accept a license granted under a foreign law to practice the profession, pursuant to Article 41(1)(a) of the Law, the licensing requirements under the foreign law shall not be lower than the requirements for practicing the profession in the Kingdom. 

Article 37:
For registering a non-Saudi consultant in the register of non-Saudi consultants, the licensee shall submit to the competent department, along with the application, the employment contract and proof of the fulfillment of the registration requirements.

Article 38:
1. The register of non-Saudi consultants shall include the consultant’s name, nationality, and contact information; the name of the licensed lawyer for whom the consultant works and his contact information; and any other information specified by the competent department. 
2. The licensee must notify the competent department of any change to the information of the consultant registered in the register of non-Saudi consultants within a period not exceeding 15 days from the date of such change. 

Article 39:
1. A non-Saudi consultant registered in the register of non-Saudi consultants shall comply with the Rules of Professional Conduct for Lawyers and the profession’s code of ethics as stipulated in the Law, these Regulations, and relevant laws. 
2. The competent department may remove the non-Saudi consultant from the register of non-Saudi consultants if he fails to satisfy the obligations stipulated in paragraph (1) of this Article. 

Article 40:
1. The licensee shall notify the competent department upon the expiration of the contractual relationship with the consultant registered in the register of non-Saudi consultants within a period not exceeding 15 days from the date of its expiration. 
2. The registration of the non-Saudi consultant shall be canceled within 30 days from the date of expiration of the contractual relationship unless another licensee submits within such period an application to register the consultant in accordance with Article 37 of these Regulations. 

PART 6: LITIGATION BY OTHER THAN LAWYERS
CHAPTER 1: LITIGATION BY AMBASSADORS OF STATES AND REPRESENTATIVES OF PUBLIC LEGAL PERSONS
Article 41:
Without prejudice to Article 18 of the Law, ambassadors of states to the Kingdom may litigate on behalf of their embassies. 

Article 42:
A public legal person shall be represented by its head or whomever he delegates from among his employees, provided that the delegated employee is a Saudi national. 

CHAPTER 2: LITIGATION ON BEHALF OF A PRIVATE LEGAL PERSON
Article 43:
Employees at the legal departments of the private legal person shall be considered the legal representatives thereof, upon authorization from the competent department and pursuant to a power of attorney by the authorized party. A special register shall be established therefor in the competent department. 

Article 44:
An application to obtain authorization for litigation on behalf of a private legal person shall be submitted to the competent department, accompanied with the documents specified by said department.

Article 45:
To obtain authorization for litigation on behalf of a private legal person, the person seeking authorization must:
1. hold a bachelor's degree in Sharia or law from a university in the Kingdom, or an equivalent thereof; and 
2. be employed by the private legal person under an employment contract in accordance with the provisions of the Labor Law. 

Article 46:
1. The person authorized to litigate on behalf of a private legal person shall comply with the following: 
a) The Rules of Professional Conduct for Lawyers and the profession’s code of ethics as stipulated in the Law, these Regulations, and relevant laws. 
b) Refrain from litigating on behalf of other than the private legal person or its subsidiary companies whom he is authorized to represent. 
c) Refrain from presenting himself as a lawyer or implying that he is a licensed lawyer.
2. The competent department may cancel the authorization of the person authorized to litigate on behalf of the private legal person and remove such person from the register if he fails to meet the obligations provided for in paragraph (1) of this Article. 

Article 47:
The authorization of the person authorized to litigate on behalf of a private legal person shall be canceled pursuant to a decision by the competent department, in the following cases: 
1. Expiration of the employment contract with the private legal person.
2. Issuance of a decision to cancel the authorization and remove the authorized person from the register. 
3. Death of the authorized person. 

PART 7: FOREIGN LAW FIRMS
CHAPTER 1: CONDITIONS AND PROCEDURES FOR LICENSING FOREIGN LAW FIRMS
Article 48:
Subject to the provisions of Article 45 of the Law, a license shall not be granted to a foreign law firm if a final judgment for an offense impinging on honor or integrity or a final decision for a grave professional misconduct is issued against such firm in any country where it operates, unless a period of not less than five years has elapsed since the end of the enforcement of such judgment or decision. 

Article 49:
In fulfilling the minimum requirement stipulated in Article 45(3) of the Law, the following must be observed: 
1. The country in which the foreign law firm has legal representations or partnerships must be economically developed, in accordance with established international indicators and standards. 
2. The legal representation or partnership in the country or region allows the law firm to practice in such country or region, and the law firm must have an office therein with at least three employees performing services of legal nature.

Article 50:
For purposes of computing the period provided for in Article 45(2) of the Law, if a foreign law firm underwent a merger or acquisition, the year of establishment of the older foreign law firm shall be considered. 

Article 51:
The partner representing the foreign law firm in the Kingdom, as provided for in Article 45(4) of the Law, must meet the following conditions: 
1. Be licensed to practice law in accordance with the provisions of the Law or any other relevant foreign law.
2. Have relevant legal experience for a period of not less than 10 years, three of which are after the issuance of the license to practice law. 
3. A final judgment for an offense impinging on honor or integrity or a final decision for a grave professional misconduct has not been issued against him in any country in which he practices unless a period of not less than five years has elapsed since the end of the enforcement of the judgment or decision. 

Article 52:
If the partner fails to establish residence in the Kingdom or if there is a desire to have him replaced, the foreign law firm must name a partner who meets the conditions and requirements stipulated in the Law and these Regulations, provided that such requirements do not affect the foreign law firm’s fulfillment of the period provided for in Article 45(4) of the Law.

Article 53:
The foreign law firm shall provide the following documents with the license application: 
1. A document authorizing the firm’s legal representative to submit the application for license to practice law in the Kingdom. 
2. The license documents of the firm’s headquarters and its other branch offices. 
3. The law practice license of the partner representing the foreign law firm in the Kingdom, along with proof of his partnership and experience. 
4. An acknowledgment that neither the foreign law firm nor the representing partner has been subject to a final judgment or decision for an offense impinging on honor or integrity or for grave professional misconduct. 
5. The necessary approvals relating to foreign investment, in accordance with relevant laws. 

Article 54:
1. A foreign law firm may not operate before obtaining membership in the Association and establishing an office.
2. The foreign law firm shall notify the competent department upon fulfillment of the requirements stipulated in paragraph (1) of this Article prior to commencing operations. 

Article 55:
The register of foreign law firms shall include the following information: 
1. Name, license, and contact information of the foreign law firm. 
2. The legal form adopted by the foreign law firm, and the basic information of the partners, if any, along with their contact information. 
3. The number of the license granted for operation in the Kingdom, and its issuance and expiration dates.
4. The address of the office where operations are conducted. 
5. Any other information specified by the competent department. 
The foreign law firm shall notify the competent department of any change to its information within a period not exceeding 15 days from the date of such change. 

Article 56:
The application for license renewal shall be submitted within 180 days prior to the expiration date and within one year following such date. The documents specified by the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee shall be attached to the application, and the applicant must sign a form acknowledging continued compliance with the conditions provided for in Article 45 of the Law.

Article 57:
If the Lawyers Admission and Registration Committee rejects the license application or license renewal, the applicant shall be notified thereof, and he may submit a new application if the grounds on which the application was rejected no longer exist. The applicant may file a grievance before the Board of Grievances within 60 days from the date of notification of the Committee's decision.

CHAPTER 2: LEGAL FORM OF THE FOREIGN LAW FIRM AND SCOPE OF PRACTICE
Article 58:
If a foreign law firm takes the form of a professional company with one or more Saudi lawyers registered in the roll of practicing lawyers, the firm must comply with the following: 
1. One of the Saudi partners or shareholders must have a relevant legal experience of not less than seven years. 
2. One of the Saudi partners or shareholders must be appointed as the manager of the firm. A non-Saudi may be appointed as a manager, provided that the Saudi partners or shareholders, licensed to practice, hold not less than one quarter of the voting power in the board of directors or the board of managers, as the case may be. 

Article 59:
For a foreign law firm operating in the form specified in Article 50(2) of the Law to provide legal consultations relating to the laws of the Kingdom, such consultations shall be provided by a lawyer listed in the roll of practicing lawyers who meets the following conditions: 
1. Works for the foreign law firm under an employment contract subject to the provisions of the Labor Law.
2. Has relevant legal experience for a period not less than three years from the date of obtaining the license.

CHAPTER 3: FOREIGN LAW FIRM OBLIGATIONS
Article 60:
To fulfill its obligations as stipulated in Article 52 of the Law, the foreign law firm must comply with the following: 
1. The number of Saudi nationals engaged in legal work in the foreign law firm shall not be less than the Saudization percentages prescribed for Saudi law firms and professional companies in accordance with the relevant laws and decisions. 
2. The foreign law firm shall develop an annual work plan for training and transfer of knowledge, which shall include the following at a minimum: 
a) Provide 20 training hours annually to each individual engaged in legal work in the firm. 
b) Adopt a policy for the secondment of Saudi employees to the foreign law firm’s headquarters or branches abroad. 
c) Provide professional technical and administrative development programs for Saudi employees. 
d) Implement co-op and training programs for university students and recent graduates in legal practice. 
e) Contribute to the organization or sponsorship of academic and professional conferences, seminars, and events in the Kingdom. 
f) Engage its Saudi employees in the operations of the foreign law firm’s units and departments, as well as in the firm’s projects in the Kingdom. 
3. The value of consultation services transferred outside the Kingdom shall not exceed 30% of the value of the total annual consultation services of the firm, excluding consultation services concerning non-Saudi laws for services relating to works outside the Kingdom. 

Article 61:
The foreign law firm shall, upon request, provide the competent department with the approved financial statements, an annual report showing its commitment to knowledge transfer and training plan, and any relevant information or reports for the purpose of verifying the firm’s compliance with its legal obligations.

CHAPTER 4: TEMPORARY LICENSE
Article 62:
The temporary license may only be granted for the provision of consultations relating to exceptional or specialized projects that are necessary for the Kingdom, and for which local expertise is lacking. In issuing a temporary license, the following provisions and procedures shall be observed: 
1. A foreign law firm may not be granted more than one temporary license for a period of one year starting from the date on which such license was obtained, except under exceptional circumstances and upon obtaining the Minister’s approval. 
2. A foreign law firm applying for a temporary license must obtain the required approvals relating to foreign investment, in accordance with relevant laws, and must meet the conditions stipulated for licensing foreign law firms, except for residency conditions and for conditions and requirements relating to the legal form under which the law firm operates in the Kingdom. 
3. The application for a temporary license shall include a letter from the agency owning the project requesting to contract with the foreign law firm, along with documents indicating that the project is exceptional or specialized.

Article 63:
A foreign law firm holding a temporary license shall comply with the following: 
1. Take the necessary measures to ensure compliance of the firm and the employees assigned to the project with the Rules of Professional Conduct for Lawyers and with the profession’s code of ethics as stipulated in the Law, these Regulations, and relevant laws, in addition to any professional laws not inconsistent therewith in the country in which the foreign law firm is licensed to operate.
2. Refrain from providing any service beyond the scope of the license. 
3. Assign at least 10% of the project’s legal services to a licensed Saudi lawyer; such percentage shall be calculated based on the value of the services. 
4. Submit a report to the competent department upon completion of the project containing the services rendered and any information requested by the competent department.

PART 8: DISCIPLINARY PROVISIONS
CHAPTER 1: DETECTION AND RECORDING OF PROFESSION-RELATED VIOLATIONS
Article 64:
Complaints and reports shall be submitted to the competent department using the designated forms and following the approved procedures. 

Article 65:
The competent department shall be responsible for monitoring, inspecting, and detecting and recording violations as well as verifying complaints and reports. To this end, it shall have the following powers: 
1. Entering the premises in order to verify the licensee’s compliance with the laws and instructions. 
2. Reviewing the necessary documents and licenses in order to verify the licensee’s compliance with the laws and instructions. 
3. Requesting a statement from the licensee regarding the alleged violation, and hearing the statements of the complainant and, when necessary, any relevant party.

Article 66:
The competent employee shall maintain integrity and confidentiality, follow approved manuals and procedures, and refrain from engaging in any activity that involves a conflict of interest. 

Article 67:
A licensed lawyer shall enable the competent employee to perform the duties entrusted thereto. 

Article 68:
1. The competent employee shall prepare a report on the alleged violation using the designated form; such report shall include the licensee’s information, a description of the violation, date of the incident, relevant evidence and presumptions, and a recommendation to close the case or refer it to investigation. 
2. The competent department shall refer the licensee for investigation or decide to close the case if no violation warranting referral to investigation is established.

CHAPTER 2: INVESTIGATION AND PROSECUTION IN PROFESSION-RELATED VIOLATIONS
Article 69:
1. The competent department shall investigate violations and shall protect the violator’s rights, including informing him with the alleged violation and enabling him to present his defense. 
2. The investigation may be conducted in person, or remotely either orally or in writing through approved electronic means, as determined by the competent department. 
3. If the licensee refuses to be interrogated or fails to appear for the investigation despite being notified, the competent department shall make a note to this effect and shall refer the violation to the disciplinary committee. 

Article 70:
The competent department shall, upon reviewing and investigating the violation, or if the investigation is not feasible, take either of the of the following decisions: 
1. Closure of the case due to insufficient evidence and notifying the licensee thereof.
2. Referral of the violation to the disciplinary committee for consideration. 

Article 71:
Experts and specialist employees and consultants who meet the requirements of registration in the roll of practicing lawyers shall represent the competent department before the disciplinary committee, and they shall be appointed pursuant to a decision by the Minister or his designee. 

CHAPTER 3: DISCIPLINARY COMMITTEE
Article 72:
The disciplinary committee shall have a secretary appointed pursuant to a decision by the Minister or his designee, to be in charge of supervising the secretariat of the committee, preparing and maintaining its minutes and decisions, making the necessary technical and administrative preparations for committee work and meetings, and performing any tasks assigned by the disciplinary committee’s chairman within the limits of his powers.

Article 73:
One or more alternate members shall be appointed pursuant to a decision by the Minister to substitute for a regular member of the disciplinary committee in case of his absence or withdrawal. 

Article 74:
Disciplinary committee meetings shall be held at the request of its chairman if needed, and may be held remotely via approved electronic means. 

Article 75:
The disciplinary committee may, while the case is being investigated or considered, order, by a reasoned decision, the temporary suspension of the licensee from practicing for a period not exceeding 90 days, renewable for a similar period, if the interest so requires. 

Article 76:
1. A member of the disciplinary committee shall recuse himself from the case if there is a conflict of interest, as in any of the following cases:
a) If he is the spouse, a relative, or an in-law up to the fourth degree of the licensee or complainant. 
b) If a dispute, enmity, or affinity exists between him and the licensee or the complainant, which may affect his impartiality. 
2. Should grounds to disqualify him from considering a disciplinary case exist and he fails to recuse himself, either party to the case may request his disqualification, and the Minister, or his designee, shall issue a decision on the request and such decision shall be deemed final. 

Article 77:
The disciplinary committee may, in exercising its functions, take the following actions: 
1. Request from the licensee and relevant entities any necessary documents. 
2. Request statements from parties related to the violation, and interrogate the licensee. 
3. Seek the assistance of experts and specialists to attend its meetings or to provide opinion. 

CHAPTER 4: DISCIPLINARY CASES
Article 78:
Disciplinary case proceedings and arguments shall be in writing, and the disciplinary committee may, on its own motion or upon the request of the competent department or the licensee, hear the statement of the licensee in-person or remotely via approved electronic means. 

Article 79:
Disciplinary sessions shall be confidential, and the disciplinary committee shall prepare a record for each session that includes details of the case, its parties, and a summary thereof.

Article 80:
If the disciplinary committee or the competent department establishes that the violation under investigation or consideration involves a criminal offense, it shall notify the competent authority, and the disciplinary committee shall decide whether to continue with the disciplinary proceedings. 

Article 81:
The disciplinary committee shall decide on the disciplinary case within a period of 60 days from the date of filing; such period may be extended for a similar period when necessary. 

Article 82:
The decision of the disciplinary committee to take disciplinary action shall include the violator’s particulars, a description of the violation and relating evidence, the reasoning for the decision, and responses to the violator’s substantive defenses. 

Article 83:
The disciplinary case shall terminate upon: 
1. issuance of a final decision against the violator by the disciplinary committee; 
2. death or incapacitation of the lawyer, or termination of the licensee’s legal personality; or
3. Striking the licensee off the roll pursuant to a final decision from the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee, unless the licensee is reinstated.

CHAPTER 5: DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS AND THEIR EFFECTS
Article 84:
Transferring a lawyer to the roll of non-practicing lawyers or reinstating him in the roll of practicing lawyers shall not preclude the initiation of disciplinary proceedings against him for any violation committed thereby. 

Article 85:
1. The court shall provide the competent department with a copy of the judgement issued against the lawyer if the punishment is a hadd or is for an offense impinging upon honor or integrity.
2. The lawyer’s name shall be stricken off from the roll of practicing lawyers and his license shall be revoked pursuant to a decision by the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee if he is sentenced to a hadd punishment or to any other punishment for an offense impinging upon honor or integrity. 

Article 86:
1. A lawyer, whose name has been stricken off the roll and whose license has been revoked due to being sentenced to a hadd punishment or to any other punishment for an offense impinging upon honor or integrity, may request from the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee his reinstatement after the lapse of five years from the completion of the sentence. 
2. The Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee shall review and decide on the reinstatement request upon verifying the fulfillment of license requirements, subject to the use of designated forms and application of approved procedures; if the request is rejected, the lawyer may reapply after the lapse of one year from the date of rejection. 
3. The procedure stipulated in paragraph (1) of this Article shall apply to a lawyer whose name has been stricken off from the roll pursuant to a final disciplinary decision by the disciplinary committee if he requests reinstatement to the roll after the lapse of the period stipulated in Article 36 of the Law. 

Article 87:
A lawyer against whom a final disciplinary decision of suspension is issued shall comply with the following: 
1. Inform the Lawyers Registration and Admission Committee of the pending services and cases in his care, contracts with his clients, associated rights and obligations, the measures to be taken thereby to reach an arrangement thereon, and the period required therefor, which shall not exceed 60 days in all cases.
2. After the end of the period referred to in paragraph (1) of this Article, the lawyer shall state the pending services and cases for which arrangements have been made along with proof thereof as well as the pending services and cases for which no arrangements have been made; if, however, he is a partner or shareholder in a professional company, he shall provide proof of the measures taken by the company in this regard and of the reassignment of the services to other licensed partners or shareholders, in accordance with relevant laws.
3. If a suspended lawyer fails to provide proof of arrangements regarding pending services and cases, the Ministry may communicate with the courts, Board of Grievances, and other competent agencies to obtain information about such cases in order to address them accordingly.
4. If a suspended lawyer fails to comply with the measures provided for in this Article, he shall be referred to investigation in order to complete disciplinary proceedings.

CHAPTER 6: IMPERSONATION OF A LAWYER
Article 88:
The following shall, in accordance with Article 37 of the Law, be deemed an impersonation of a lawyer and practicing law in violation of the provisions of the Law: 
1. Practicing law without a license, unless exempted under Article 18 of the Law. 
2. Practicing law after choosing to suspend his practice or being suspended, or after the revocation of his license. 
3. Setting up an office for the practice of law without a license.
4. Claiming to be a lawyer or advertising himself as such without a license. 
5. Providing legal consultations through electronic platforms without a license. 

PART 9: FINAL PROVISIONS
Article 89:
Procedures provided for in the Law and these Regulations may be carried out via electronic means approved by the Ministry, including notification and access to documents.

Article 90:
The competent department shall, upon obtaining the Minister's approval, issue the procedural manuals and forms necessary for the implementation of the Law and these Regulations.
